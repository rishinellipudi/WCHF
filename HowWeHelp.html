How We Help

At WCHF, our mission is to educate the public about historical war crimes and advocate for justice. Even though our project is too small of a scale to take any action or cause change, understanding is the first step toward change and we want to educate students about the reality of war crimes and raise awareness.

The Gap We Fill/What We Do

For this project we have a few key objectives:
- Educating: We will potentially be looking into building lesson plans about War Crimes that can be incorporated into high school english or history classes. Though they are talked about somewhat, these lesson plans will be specific and bring into light the hidden destructions and pain war crimes have caused. They will help students understand how to prevent injustice in the future
- Advocating: Calling attention to the need for justice and accountability
- Raising Awareness: Creating conversations that help educate and inspire others to take action

Facts About War Crimes

- Thought less severe than in the past, over 100000 civilizans have been killed in modern conflict as a result of war crimes since 2000
-Under the Geneva Conventions, war crimes include actions like targeting civilians, torture, and using prohibited weapons such as chemical or biological agents
-As of 2021, fewer than 10% of individuals accused of war crimes have been prosecuted, despite law regulations
-The aftermath of war crimes often leaves generations of survivors dealing with physical and psychological trauma
-Sexual violence is often used as a weapon of war, with an estimated 250,000 to 500,000 women raped in conflicts like the Bosnian War and the Rwandan Genocide

Historical Examples

These are some of the most famous and severe war crimes:
- Nuremberg Trials (1945-1949): 24 Nazi leaders were tried for war crimes after WWII, with 12 sentenced to death for atrocities including the Holocaust
- The Holocaust (1941-1945): The Nazis killed 6 million Jews and millions of others, marking the largest genocide in history
- Rwandan Genocide (1994): 800,000 people were killed in 100 days by Hutu extremists in one of the fastest genocides in history
-Unit 731 (1937-1945): The Japanese Army conducted deadly human experiments, killing thousands during WWII
- Khmer Rouge (1975-1979): 1.7 million Cambodians died from executions, forced labor, and starvation under Pol Pot's regime
- The Bosnian War (1992-1995): Over 8,000 Bosniaks were executed in the Srebrenica massacre by Bosnian Serb forces
- Armenian Genocide (1915-1923): The Ottoman Empire killed 1.5 million Armenians through executions and forced deportations
